This project develops a resilience-oriented restoration framework that treats selected hyperscale data centers as coordinated infrastructure assets rather than passive electricity loads during extreme weather disruptions.

The system integrates:

• Distribution network reconfiguration under uncertain damage and repair timelines  
• Explicit modeling of data center behind-the-meter resources (UPS, storage, controllable load)  
• Survivability-constrained restoration optimization  
• Graph Neural Network–based reinforcement learning for scalable decision-making  
• Scenario-based hurricane outage modeling with stochastic repair processes  
• Value of Lost Load (VoLL)–weighted objective formulation  

The restoration environment models:

• Benchmark distribution test systems under hurricane-driven line failures  
• Time-varying component availability and access constraints  
• Critical-facility prioritization (hospitals, water, emergency services)  
• Coordinated load restoration with data center operational envelopes  
• Resilience metrics including VoLL-weighted unserved energy, time-to-restore critical loads, and restoration trajectory performance  

Key technical contributions include:

• First systematic evaluation of data centers as “restoration anchors” within a restoration-oriented decision framework  
• Explicit balancing of community recovery objectives and data center survivability constraints  
• Topology-aware GNN encoder for structured network state representation  
• Quantification of resilience value relative to conventional and self-preservation-only restoration strategies  
• Integration of digital infrastructure into distribution-level emergency operations modeling  

Impact:

• Demonstrated reduction in VoLL-weighted unserved energy relative to conventional restoration  
• Accelerated restoration of critical community loads under severe outage scenarios  
• Identified operational conditions under which data center flexibility enhances grid recovery without compromising internal reliability  
• Provided structured decision framework for utilities managing rapidly growing AI-driven hyperscale loads  

The framework reframes hyperscale digital infrastructure from grid stressors to programmable resilience resources and establishes a scalable modeling approach for integrating large controllable loads into utility restoration planning.
