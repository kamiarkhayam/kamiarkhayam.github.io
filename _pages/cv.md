## Research Focus

Machine learning for large-scale infrastructure systems, with emphasis on:

• Graph neural networks and reinforcement learning  
• Power system reliability and restoration  
• Uncertainty quantification and risk-aware planning  
• Energy system optimization and decarbonization modeling  

Work integrates high-dimensional simulation, graph representation learning, and stochastic optimization for operational and long-term infrastructure decision support.

---

## Research & Professional Experience

**Pacific Gas and Electric Company (PG&E)** — Research Collaborator  
Developed temporal graph neural networks for structure-level failure localization and root-cause prediction in transmission systems, enabling probabilistic risk forecasting for predictive maintenance planning.

**Maryland Department of Transportation (MDOT SHA)** — Research Intern  
Designed statewide GIS-based multi-criteria optimization framework for drainage infrastructure prioritization under climate stressors; built operational decision-support tools adopted for maintenance allocation.

**University of Virginia — Computational Analytics for Smart Systems Lab**  
Research Assistant  

Selected contributions:

• Designed MAGNN-A2C, a topology-aware multi-agent reinforcement learning framework for post-hurricane distribution restoration on a >16,000-node real network; reduced outage cost by 18–23% and avoided ~$70–120M per event.  

• Developed multi-fidelity GNN surrogates for probabilistic power flow and chance-constrained OPF; reduced violation probability from 0.23 to 0.06 with up to 96% computational savings.  

• Built high-resolution capacity expansion model evaluating data center growth and building electrification; demonstrated $200–350M annual residential bill reductions with limited incremental generation needs.  

• Integrated GCAM-USA with large-ensemble Monte Carlo and neural surrogates for deep uncertainty analysis; achieved R² up to 0.98 and identified dominant decarbonization drivers.
